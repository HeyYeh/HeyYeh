Rage, Flower Thrower

Behind the stitch:

This was the first stitch-ified Banksy that I designed and made, wanting to play around with silhouettes and shape over internal detail. Banksy is fantastic in showing an awful lot with a small colour palette, so it made sense to try and make something based off of one of his most famous pieces! The original art was found in Jerusalem in 2005 (it’s also huge, and I believe still there today), but it appears in stitch form now thanks to Pyxel.

This was, if I remember correctly, actually the first time that I used Pyxel to design one of these, and it was a bit of a learning process in those early days. It’s a very clean, simple program, but everything has some degree of a learning curve, after all. Surprisingly too, the main body of the protester wasn’t that difficult to put together in its basic form, but the flowers, on the other hand? The flowers in Banksy’s original work are very detailed, and small, and these two things don’t translate easily to pixel art that is a fraction of the size of the original. It took a few drafts until i was happy with the flowers as a whole (they still seem a little big to me, but what can you do?), and then I started stitching away! As with anything I’ve designed on here, you are welcome to make it yourself and all I ask is a shout of some kind and for you to show me what you’ve made!

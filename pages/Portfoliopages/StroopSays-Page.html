Behind the game:

This was a quick project to quickly make a proof of concept for a silly idea that I've had for a while. As you can imagine, it's based on the classic Simon Says boards game (this one) in which a series of colours flash and you have to repeat the series for points; it gets longer each round, and so it gets more challenging. The Stroop test, meanwhile, is a pretty well known test of either reaction time or global vs local visual processing depending on who you ask. It also featured in Dr. Kawashima's Brain Training on the DS as a way to improve your memory. Fun fact about that, there has been some research that showed this game and the exercises in it helped to prevent dementia and Alzheimer's (though this is, naturally, disputed - long story), but Nintendo of America didn't support these claims because, and I quote, "They are in the entertainment industry".

Aaaaaaanyway, I liked the idea of making Simon Says Stroop-ified because it strips the game down a little further. For example, in the original Simon Says you could do well if you had a great memory for spatial positioning (the colours never move) OR good memory of colour. In Stroop Says, the colours move between rounds and require you to focus on visual detail rather than the semantics of the word, so it's more challenging and tests for different abilities than the original game. I can't really do any studies testing the nature of spatial memory over colour memory (there's a scientific Journal called Color that does a better job of that than I ever could), but it's kinda cool at least. Sometimes, that's more than enough reason to make something!

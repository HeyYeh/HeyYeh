Earthquake - For The Girl

Behind the video:

I don’t have as much to say about this one, though it’s probably the one that needs the most explanation…

In essence, this was an experiment with a more natural, more personal style of music video. The whole band had this strong, great personality and we wanted to make something to let their audience really feel it, and how better to feel it than following them on a day-in-their-life? Okay, sure, actually talking to them would be easier but we also made videos like that for their social media so… Pipe down, smarty pants.

The pre-production for this one was nice and relaxed, and the main focus was on arranging the activities that the band went and did. Everything in the video was planned out (shock horror that it wasn’t a real day in their lives, I know), and we actually ended up leaving out an entire segment where they went to an arcade because of the sheer amount of footage that we obtained. No surprise either because we had the whole band filming each other for filler shots as well as our own cameras, which gave a cool, raw feel.

A couple of fun facts: I make a brief appearance in this video looking like Meep, and one of the band members gets a tattoo of my surname in the video due to an unfortunately lost bet on their part…

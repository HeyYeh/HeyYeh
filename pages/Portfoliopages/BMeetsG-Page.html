Boy Meets Girl

Behind the stitch:

This piece is based off one of the lesser known Banksy pieces of the same name (or at least probably the same name, there aren’t many cases with definitive names for his/her/their work). This was less focused on backstitching in general (thin black outlining) so I got to design this in Pyxel and overlay a grid using Gimp afterwards so that I could track what I needed to stitch… That is, when I get around to stitching it. I haven’t actually made this one just yet, I just wanted to make the pattern and have it available if the mood strikes.

There isn’t too much to say regarding the design, really. Just like the other Banksy pieces, I pretty much did this from eye and tried to get as close a match as possible. I quite like working with silhouettes like this because it really forces you to get the shape and global image right, instead of relying on shading or outlining to provide detail. However, this does make drawing flowers incredibly frustrating so, much like the Flower Thrower piece, I had to make the flowers here from scratch. In spite of that, I like how it turned out, but I wish Banksy started using much blockier flowers in general… As is the case with any of the patterns on here, feel free to use it without asking for permission, but I would love if you give me a shout out wherever you post it, and I would love it even more if you show me the finished piece!

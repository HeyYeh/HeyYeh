Peter Rabbit

Behind the stitch:

This is a very pleasant little design, created based off of this classic Beatrix Potter illustration. Because the backstitching (the black outline) was a big part of the design, I used the dedicated cross-stitch web app at craftdesignonline.com. For less backstitch heavy pieces, I prefer to use Pyxel purely because I like treating cross-stitch designs like regular pixel art!

For this particular piece, I wanted to practice shading on a relatively small project, and a friend is a huge fan of Peter Rabbit so I saw an opportunity for a gift! There were a lot of inherent challenges involved in making the blending in the fur look at least somewhat natural, mainly because Beatrix Potter takes a fairly detailed approach to shading and colour in her illustrations (or at least, much more detailed than pixel art allows). In fact, the shading is the only thing that really gives the head and body any kind of ‘shape’, making them a bit more than just a rough circle blob of colour. It took a few drafts, and me asking many people ‘does this look right to you?’ before I was happy with a final design, but I’m pretty pleased with how it turned out! As with anything I post on here, if you want to make this yourself you are more than welcome to without asking my permission. I would love if you point people here or give me a shout out in some form, and I would love it even more if you show me what you made, but beyond that just have fun!

Also as a side note, Beatrix Potter was an absolutely amazing artist in every sense. I am a huge fan of biological drawings, and this is actually how Potter started out in the world of art. I will post a few pictures of her pieces below, but I urge you to go and explore her talent in more depth if you get a chance!

Meta Knit Jumper

Behind the stitch:

This is a very rough outline of an idea made on request of the very talented knitting team at Women’s Weekly when I was enjoying some time there. The general idea was to create a sort of meta-knit jumper that showed hands knitting the lower half of it - the idea of something very self-creating like this was fresh in my mind due to just finishing work on a similarly themed short film. This was naively made purely in Gimp using a grid that I believe I found on Google images that I stretched horizontally (knitting patterns use rectangles rather than squares). I say naively to protect my feelings of course; this was just a terrible choice. It meant using the fill paint tool to actually design it, and it could have been much quicker if I knew then what I know now.

I do have to confess though… I am not a knitter, and I really have no idea how viable this pattern is as I moved away after passing it to the knitting team (though they were happy to receive it anyway). I know that this makes for a big pattern, I tried smaller and wasn’t satisfied with how it looked, and the colour is deliberately quite bold because my knowledge of knitting yarn colours is very, very limited. You are more than welcome to change the pattern, colour or otherwise, to suit your needs if you so wish, but do let me know if you do so that I can learn from people with greater knitting knowledge!

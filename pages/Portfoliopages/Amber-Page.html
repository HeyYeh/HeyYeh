Amber

Behind the video:

This was the very first video project that I worked on, and it was an absolute crash course in a lot of things. My main role here was writing, and I wanted to explore some element of psychology while keeping the story simple and contained (as short films often benefit from). After a lot of drafts, I settled on looking into an unusual little love triangle involving Stockholm syndrome (a captive falling in love with their captor), and Lima syndrome (captor falling in love with the captive). I was present for and involved in every step of the production process though, giving me a taste for playing a much bigger part in these jobs for pretty much every other project I took part in.

As I said, this was the first video that I had a hand in making, and there were hiccups that we could not have predicted along the way. It fell a little short of the vision that we had in mind for it at first, but it did earn a few film festival selections, as well as winning a city wide 'best short' award. It is so far from perfect, but it was a huge learning experience that has proven invaluable in my approach to other projects, even outside of film.

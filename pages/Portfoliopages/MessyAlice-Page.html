Minimalist Alice

Behind the stitch:

I am a big fan of Alice in Wonderland (I can see three copies of it from where I’m sitting right now), and I imagine it had a pretty big influence on me growing up. With this in mind, I’m actually surprised that I haven’t done more little things based on it! This one is a pattern that I made based on this watercolour painting, made by DashesOfColor on Etsy. I loved how free flowing it felt, colours going beyond outlines, but it maintained a nice minimalism to it too, so, naturally, I wanted to see if it would translate well to pixel art/cross stitch! Due to the use of backstitching (thin black lines), I designed this in the dedicated cross-stitch web app at craftdesignonline.com.

This had some interesting difficulties in converting to pixel art too, purely because I was very eager not to make it too large - the larger the piece in total, the more stitches/pixels you use meaning the more detail you can add. I felt that making it huge but super close to the source material would be a little pointless, so I took the challenge and ended up with, what I think, is a decent compromise into stitch form. Either way though, huge credit has to go to the very talented original artist linked above!

What is London Hire Ian Day?

Ah, the day of the year that we know one person in particular has been waiting for! Who would have thought that what started as a vague idea to stand out in the hiring pool has become a whole re-write of a webpage? One way or another, this London based creative copywriter is certainly eager to please, delight, and - you better believe - write his little socks off. That was a joke, his feet are certainly average sized.  
 

History of London Hire Ian Day

As a young enterprising sort, Ian decided to write and create a short film not long after attending university. It was, as the French say, 'not good at all'. As most first attempts are it was destined for failure, but what do we do when we fall off the horse? We get back on and we show that horse who’s boss by riding it until it is the one that is falling off the horse. Or something.
 
This is to say, Ian was - almost foolishly - persistent. He started writing pitches for strange music video ideas to bands, musicians, and music labels, which spiralled into a frelance career of concepting and creating videos for musicians. But, like a vampire at a blood sausage festival, he had only gotten a taste of what would prove to be his lifelong love: writing to entertain.
 
Not long after leaving university, Ian wanted to capitalise on that need to write but had no idea at the time how to do it. I mean, writing for a living? Was he a wizard or something? The closest he found was working in the editorial department at a huge science publisher, and it was better than expected. He got to edit what scientists wrote, proofread interesting articles, and write summaries of complex papers... But it wasn't writing to entertain, was it? Sure, he got the administrative and organisational skills necessary to succeed in copywriting, but he wasn't doing what he loved. 
 
He kept aggresively expanding his freelance sidegig of film making and, eventually, that caught the attention of a London based advertising agency. He went in as one of just two creatives, meaning that he was both a copywriter and creative strategist as needed. Sometimes both at the same time. He got to create fun work for immense brands like Google and Intel on a day to day basis, across an immense number of topics and formats. Social media, videogames, pitches, strategy documents, and even sad clown backstories at one point (but that's a story for another day). 
 
We celebrate February 17th as London Hire Ian day because this is the arbitrary date that Ian had time to write this fun little article. 

You can browse a short portfolio of some of his work here, or look at the favourite videos he has written on his directing page here. 

London Hire Ian Day related holidays

National Videogame Writing Day


Narrative and writing within videogames is a truly unique art form. In many ways, narrative is at the mercy of gameplay, which might be why Ian is so fascinated by it as a format. In his own time, he likes to code and write games for fun, and is a member of the writing team in the big ol' Skyrim mod Skywind.

National Special Effects Day


Building special effects for film is the process of researching, designing, and problem solving things no one has done before. From making a man sweat green to creating gigantic functional angel wings, Ian occasionally dabbles in SFX work for himself and brands like Boots and Young's.
